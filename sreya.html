The Mind Behind

Hello, I'm Sreya — currently in my third year of Psychology at PSG College of Arts and Science. I'm particularly drawn to Neuropsychology, Cognitive Psychology, and Indian Psychology. This vast field has always fascinated me, and I'm eager to delve deeper into how the brain shapes behaviour, memory, and identity.

I believe that understanding the human mind is one of the most meaningful pursuits there is — from the firing of a single synapse to the patterns of an entire culture's collective thought.

Beyond academics, I'm a reader, a traveller, and someone who finds wonder in the infinite number of things left to explore in this world.

// Dance & Discipline

Bharathanatyam —
Seven Years in Motion

"Dance is not only something I enjoy — it is something that has taught me everything about discipline, time-management, and having a passion that fuels you from within."

A professionally trained Bharathanatyam dancer with seven years of performing experience, Sreya completed her Arangetram in 2016 — the formal debut performance marking a dancer's graduation into the classical tradition.

The rigour of classical Indian dance has shaped her academic approach too: the same attention to detail required to perfect an adavu carries into her study of psychological theory.
